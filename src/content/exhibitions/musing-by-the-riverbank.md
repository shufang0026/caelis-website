Caelis Galería is pleased to present *Musing by the Riverbank*, the inaugural large-scale contemporary art group exhibition at our new M50 space. The exhibition brings together fifteen artists from Spain, Argentina, the United States, Norway, the United Kingdom, France, Japan and Korea, creating a rich dialogue across continents and artistic traditions.

**Participating Artists:**
August Vilella (Spain) · Arol (Argentina) · Adam Handler (USA) · Cesc Abad (Spain) · Debbie Reda (Italy) · Esther Ziher (Poland) · Fanny Brodar (Norway) · GONHDO (South Korea) · Jacob Vilató (Spain) · Jaime Sancorlo (Spain) · Keigo Nakamura (Japan) · Niall Campbell (UK) · Skolyshev (Russia) · Sniqus (Spain) · Xavi Carbonell (Spain)

As the ancient Greek philosopher Heraclitus wrote, *no man ever steps in the same river twice*. Everything flows, and nothing stays. The exhibition takes this profound observation as its point of departure, inviting viewers to contemplate the passage of time and the nature of existence while immersed in the beauty of contemporary art.

The diverse range of artistic voices represented in *Musing by the Riverbank* reflects Caelis Galería's ongoing commitment to presenting work that speaks across cultural boundaries — from the figurative and fantastical to the abstract and introspective. Each artist brings their own response to the exhibition's themes, creating a multi-layered exploration of what it means to be present, to change, and to endure.
