Coinciding with the 50th anniversary of the establishment of diplomatic relations between China and Spain, Caelis Galería, in cooperation with the Spanish Consulate in Shanghai and NOÉ BCN China, travelled to Suzhou from September 21st to 24th to take part in the Fifth Grand Canal Cultural and Tourism Expo, presenting 22 works by influential Spanish artists and contributing to the development of Spanish contemporary art in China.

## About the Expo

With the core of promoting canal culture and driving urban development, this year's Grand Canal Culture and Tourism Expo continues to move forward on the basis of previous experience, arranging six major sections: a night walk on the Bao Belt Bridge, opening ceremony, a night tour of Jinji Lake, exhibitions & display, themed forum, and interaction.

## Featured Artists

With the joint efforts of all parties, the exhibition featured the work of renowned Spanish surrealist **Salvador Dalí**, "The Hand. The Remorse of Conscience", as well as the works of six important artists: **August Puig**, **Carlos Tirado**, **Joaquim Falco**, **Àlex SantaFe**, **Martín Carral**, and **Carmen Anzanodeng**.

Caelis Galería also selected the best works of the represented artists **Arol**, **Debbie Reda**, **Jaime Sancorlo**, **Sniqus**, **TKM.XOXO**, and **@Gonhdo** to showcase young Spanish contemporary artists, along with a selection of Chinese works inspired by Spanish-style architecture in the same exhibition, presenting a rich visual feast.

## Looking Forward

As the gallery adheres to the concept of "integrating the East and the West with the past and the present," in the future, Caelis Galería will continue to explore the charm of young Spanish contemporary artists. We'll not forget the original intention, do a good job of the bridge between Chinese and Western art exchanges, to bring you more excellent Spanish art works, and create a comfortable art world.
