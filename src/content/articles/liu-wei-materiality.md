Liu Wei stands as one of the most compelling voices in contemporary Chinese art, his practice spanning installation, sculpture, video, and painting with remarkable versatility and conceptual rigor. Born in 1972 in Beijing, Liu came of age during the most dramatic period of urban transformation in human history—a context that has profoundly shaped his artistic vision.

## The Architecture of Transformation

At the heart of Liu Wei's work lies a fascination with the materials and structures of urban life. His installations frequently employ construction materials—plywood, steel, concrete, glass—in ways that reveal their inherent qualities while simultaneously commenting on their role in shaping the modern cityscape.

His early works, created during the rapid expansion of Beijing ahead of the 2008 Olympics, captured the anxiety and energy of a city in flux. Demolition sites became both subject and material source, as Liu incorporated salvaged materials directly into his installations, creating works that embodied the physical reality of urban change.

## Scale and Perception

Liu Wei's large-scale installations challenge our spatial perception, creating environments where familiar materials become unfamiliar, where the monumental feels intimate and the everyday becomes strange. His work asks us to look again at the built environment we inhabit daily—so often taken for granted—and to consider the social, economic, and political forces that shape it.

The relationship between individual experience and collective urban space runs throughout his practice. How does a city shape consciousness? What happens to identity when the physical environment is constantly being remade? These are questions Liu Wei's work poses with both subtlety and power.

## New Directions

In recent years, Liu Wei's practice has expanded to incorporate digital fabrication and new materials, reflecting the evolving nature of urban experience in the 21st century. His latest works engage with questions of automation, artificial intelligence, and the increasingly blurred boundary between physical and virtual spaces.

Yet throughout these formal developments, a consistent philosophical thread runs through Liu Wei's practice: a commitment to material honesty, a sensitivity to the relationship between human perception and physical environment, and a deep engagement with the forces of change that define our contemporary moment.
